Differential Swerve Drive

Project Magnemite

In just four weeks, a team of three other Mechatronics Engineering students and I have accomplished the unthinkable - designing and building the world's first-ever LEGO EV3 powered differential swerve drive.

Small. Light. Compact.
 You could even put it in your backpack and take it from class to class. We wanted to show that anything is possible with LEGO.

Features:

  • A lightweight laser-cut acrylic + FDM printed chassis, SLA printed gears and custom TPU wheel grips
  • PID Control and Path Following utilizing a gyroscopic sensor, motor encoders and ultrasonic sensors
  • Top Speed of 6 ft/s
  • Overall Weight: 1.5kg

Project Magnemite was designed in SOLIDWORKS and programmed using RobotC.

Team 610: FRC Team

Aeolus

Our team made waves in FRC by creating a one-of-a-kind robot architecture featuring an innovative pneumatic linear punching mechanism. Our commitment to innovation and problem-solving allowed us to stand out in the competition and ultimately achieve success.

Features:

  • Pneumatically powered linear punching mechanism capable of shooting 17+ ft with 45 deg of angle variation
  • Climber system capable of lifting entire robot (~100lbs)
  • Auto-Targeting that uses PID Tuning and Limelight Vision
  • Fully Automated Sequences from intaking to shooting using Optical Sensors, Proximity Sensors, Hall-Effect Sensors and Limit Switches

Awards:

  • Finalists at FRC Ontario Provincial Championship
  • Excellence in Engineering Award at University of Waterloo District Event
  • Event Winners at Humber College District Event
  • Quality Award at Humber College District Event

3D Driving Simulator

FRC DriveSim

Experience FRC driving virtually with DriveSim, the first-person 3D simulator built with Unity 3D and C#. Perfect your skills with PS4 and Xbox One controller support. Get behind the glass and take control!

DriveSim revolutionizes FRC training by providing drive teams the flexibility to practice virtually, anytime and anywhere. With an immersive first-person view, the simulator replicates the field of view of real matches, enabling drivers to train and prepare more effectively. With realistic controls, simulated computer vision, functional climber systems, and a live timer, DriveSim's physics-integrated simulator offers a true-to-life FRC driving experience.
