Differential Swerve Drive

Project Magnemite

During a span of four weeks, a team comprised of three Mechatronics Engineering students and myself joined forces to create the world's first differential swerve drive powered by LEGO EV3. Click below to find out more.

Small. Light. Compact.
 You could even put it in your backpack and take it from class to class. We wanted to show that anything is possible with LEGO.

Features:

  • A lightweight laser-cut acrylic + FDM printed chassis, SLA printed gears and custom TPU wheel grips
  • PID Control and Path Following utilizing a gyroscopic sensor, motor encoders and ultrasonic sensors
  • Top Speed of 6 ft/s
  • Overall Weight: 1.5kg

Project Magnemite was designed in SOLIDWORKS, programmed in RobotC.

Team 610: FRC Team

Aeolus

This unique robot architechure was the one of a kind featuring a pneuamtically powered linear puncher with variable power and angle (45 degrees). fully automated sequences from intaking to shooting and was an Ontario Provincial Championship Finalist, Excellence in Engineering Award and Quality Award winner.

Features:

  • Linear Puncher capable of shooting 17+ ft with 45 deg of angle variation
  • Climber system capable of lifting entire robot (~100lbs)
  • Auto-Targeting with PID Tuning using Limelight Vision
  • Fully Automated Sequences from intaking to shooting using an array of sensors including: Optical Sensors, Proximity Sensors, Hall-Effect Sensors and Limit Switches
